The Interview Guarantee Explained: How It Works (And What Counts)

Published Dec 15, 2024 · 13 min read

The guarantee is simple: qualifying technical interviews within 100 days based on your plan (Essential: 3, Standard: 5, Premium: 7), or you're eligible for a refund.

But the devil's in the details, right? Most guarantees are structured to never actually pay out. Insurance companies make billions on this model. So let's break down exactly what counts, what doesn't, and why this actually works.

What Qualifies as an Interview?

Not all "interviews" are created equal. A 15-minute phone screen with a recruiter who asks "tell me about yourself" doesn't count. Here's what does:

Qualifying Interview Criteria (All Must Be Met):

  • Technical Component: Must include coding challenge, system design, or technical deep-dive
  • Duration: Minimum 30 minutes scheduled time
  • Real Company: Verifiable company with legitimate job posting (not recruiting agencies)
  • Role Alignment: Matches your target level (junior/mid/senior) within ±1 level
  • Documentation: Calendar invite, email confirmation, or interview recording as proof

Why these criteria? Because we're guaranteeing meaningful opportunities, not spam. A 15-minute call where they "want to learn more about you" is not an interview. It's a screening. We don't count those.

What Doesn't Count

Let's be explicit about what we exclude:

  • Recruiter screens: Initial 15-20 min calls that don't discuss technical skills
  • Agency pitches: Third-party recruiters "gauging interest" for unnamed clients
  • Informational interviews: Coffee chats with no formal hiring process
  • Mismatched roles: If you're applying for senior roles, a junior position doesn't count (and vice versa)
  • Scam companies: MLMs, "commission-only," unpaid "trial periods"

We've seen candidates rack up 20+ "interviews" that were actually just recruiter intake calls. That's not what we're selling. If it doesn't test your technical skills, it doesn't count.

The 100-Day Clock

Day 1 starts when you pay your upfront fee. Not when you "feel ready." Not when you finish your portfolio. The clock starts immediately.

Why? Because without a hard deadline, people procrastinate forever. We've had beta members say "I'm not ready yet" for 8 months. The deadline forces action. And historically, 87% hit the goal by Day 95.

Why Multiple Interviews Instead of 1 Offer?

We could guarantee an offer. But that would be dishonest. Here's why:

1. We can't control whether you accept the offer. If we guarantee an offer and you get one for $85K but want $120K, you'll reject it. Did we fail? Technically no, but you'd be unhappy.

2. Offers depend on your interview performance. We can prep you relentlessly. We can get you in the room. But if you freeze during the coding challenge, that's on you. Harsh? Maybe. But it's honest.

3. Multiple interviews = statistical certainty of offers. Historical data: candidates with 5+ technical interviews convert at 68% offer rate. Most get multiple offers, not just one. The goal isn't "an offer." It's "your choice of offers."

Real Conversion Data (542 Members):

  • 5+ interviews → 68% get ≥1 offer
  • 8+ interviews → 89% get ≥1 offer
  • 12+ interviews → 96% get ≥2 offers

Average interviews per member who hit guarantee: 9.3

So when we get you your guaranteed interviews, we're not barely scraping by. We're getting you into offer territory with high probability.

What Happens If You Don't Hit Your Goal by Day 100?

You have until Day 180 to request a refund. Why the extra 80 days?

Interview scheduling lag. You might hit 5 interviews scheduled by Day 98, but 2 of them happen on Days 105 and 112 because companies move slow. We don't want to penalize you for corporate bureaucracy.

The refund window also accounts for:

  • Holidays (companies freeze hiring in Dec/Jan)
  • Offer delays (some companies take 3-4 weeks to extend offers)
  • Multi-round processes (final rounds can push past Day 100)

But here's the key: If you want a refund on Day 101, you can request it. You don't have to wait until Day 180. The window just gives you flexibility.

What If You Get Hired Before Day 100?

You still owe the success fee. The guarantee is about interviews, not speed. If you get an offer on Day 40, congratulations. You'll pay the success percentage when you start the job.

Example (Standard Plan):

  • You pay $997 upfront
  • You get 3 interviews by Day 60, accept $140K offer
  • Success fee: $140K × 10% = $14,000
  • Total cost: $997 + $14,000 = $14,997

You didn't hit 5 interviews, but you got the outcome (a job). No refund. The guarantee protects you from no interviews, not from early success.

Can You Game The System?

People ask this. "What if I just bomb every interview on purpose to get a refund?"

Sure. You could. But why would you? You've spent 100 days working on portfolio projects, prepping for interviews, and building skills. You've invested your time. Arguably more valuable than $997.

Also, we track interview performance. If you're consistently bombing technical questions you demonstrably know (based on your project work), that's a red flag. We reserve the right to deny refunds for intentional sabotage. It's in the fine print.

But in 18 months and 542 members, we've had zero cases of this. People who get interviews want offers. The incentives align.
