# 100K Pathway Sales Training Guide

Internal use only. Prepared from the current public site copy in `about`, `how-it-works`, `pricing`, `faq`, `refund`, `case-studies`, `apply`, and `terms`.

## 1. The Offer in One Sentence

100K Pathway helps experienced tech professionals get qualifying interviews within 100 days by handling applications, improving candidate positioning, and preparing them to interview well, with refund protection if the interview guarantee is not met.

## 2. Core Positioning

### What we sell

- Done-for-you job applications
- Resume and LinkedIn optimization
- Interview preparation
- Mentorship and accountability
- Salary negotiation support on higher plans
- Interview guarantee with refund protection

### What we do not sell

- A guaranteed job offer
- A guaranteed exact salary
- Immigration or visa outcomes
- A beginner coding bootcamp
- Passive "set it and forget it" career help

## 3. The Simple Pitch

Use this when a prospect asks, "What exactly do you do?"

"We are not a traditional career coach. We do the high-friction parts of the job search with you: positioning, applications, interview prep, and ongoing support. The promise is not vague advice. The promise is a specific interview guarantee in 100 days, with refund protection if we do not deliver the guaranteed number of qualifying interviews."

## 4. Ideal Customer Profile

### Best-fit prospect

- Has `2+ years` of tech experience
- Is unemployed, underemployed, or stuck below their target level
- Can already do the work, but is struggling to convert into interviews
- Wants help breaking through ATS filters and weak positioning
- Is willing to do real work for 100 days
- Can respond quickly to interview opportunities
- Has legal work authorization in the target country

### Poor-fit prospect

- Brand new to tech
- Cannot code or cannot perform in technical interviews yet
- Wants guaranteed employment instead of guaranteed interviews
- Wants a low-effort or passive outcome
- Is casually browsing while already happily employed
- Is unlikely to attend sessions or stay responsive

## 7. Guarantee and Refund Rules

### Approved explanation

- We guarantee qualifying interviews, not job offers.
- If the prospect does not receive the guaranteed number of qualifying interviews within 100 days, they can request a refund of the upfront fee under the refund policy.
- Public refund policy currently states a `$250 admin fee` applies.
- Success fee only applies if the candidate accepts a qualifying offer.

### What counts as a qualifying interview

Use this wording closely:

- Full-time W-2 employment position
- Base salary of `$80,000+`
- Company with `20+ employees`
- Role matches the candidate's target role and level
- Candidate has legal work authorization in the target country
- Interview is scheduled and confirmed by the employer

### Non-negotiable caution

Never say:

- "We guarantee you a job."
- "We guarantee you a $100K offer."
- "You will definitely make your money back."

Instead say:

- "We guarantee qualifying interviews, and we support you aggressively through the hiring process."

## 8. Discovery Questions for the Rep

Ask these in the first call or fit conversation.

1. "How long have you been actively looking?"
2. "How many applications have you submitted in the last 30 days?"
3. "What kind of roles are you targeting?"
4. "How many years of hands-on tech experience do you have?"
5. "What usually breaks down for you: getting interviews, passing interviews, or closing offers?"
6. "Are you unemployed, underemployed, or employed but stuck?"
7. "What salary band are you targeting?"
8. "Do you have legal work authorization where you want to work?"
9. "Can you commit to a structured 100-day process and respond quickly to opportunities?"
10. "Do you want lower upfront risk, more support, or the strongest possible push toward senior roles?"

## 9. Qualification Framework

### Green lights

- `2+ years` relevant experience
- Real urgency
- Has been applying without enough traction
- Understands the difference between interviews and offers
- Can commit time and follow-through
- Comfortable investing upfront for a structured outcome

### Yellow flags

- Wants the cheapest option immediately
- Has unrealistic salary expectations
- Has weak urgency
- Is vague about role target or market
- Has interview anxiety but no willingness to practice

### Red flags

- Wants a guaranteed job
- Refuses to participate in prep or sessions
- Says they will ignore interviews that are "not perfect"
- Has no legal work authorization for the target market
- Has less than 2 years of relevant experience

## 11. Objection Handling

### "Your success fee is expensive."

Response:

"It is outcome-linked. We only earn that fee if you accept a qualifying offer. The model is designed so our incentives stay aligned with yours. No offer, no success fee."

### "Why do I pay upfront if there is also a success fee?"

Response:

"The upfront fee covers the work we start immediately: positioning, application execution, prep, and support. The success fee only happens if the process leads to an accepted qualifying offer."

### "Can I just do this myself?"

Response:

"You can, and many people try. The issue is not whether it is possible. The issue is whether you want to keep carrying the full workload yourself while the market stays competitive. We reduce friction, increase consistency, and create accountability."

### "Do you guarantee a job?"

Response:

"No. We guarantee qualifying interviews. Offers still depend on interview performance, market conditions, and your choices. What we do is get you into real processes and help you perform well inside them."

### "What if I do not hit the guarantee?"

Response:

"If you meet the program requirements and still do not receive the guaranteed number of qualifying interviews by day 100, the refund policy gives you protection through day 180."

### "I am not sure I am ready."

Response:

"That depends on what 'ready' means. If you can do the work but are stuck getting traction, this is designed for that gap. If you still need to learn fundamentals from scratch, you are probably early."

### "I only want the cheapest plan."

Response:

"We can start with the plan that matches your risk tolerance, but the better question is what level of support you actually need to hit the outcome. For most qualified mid-level candidates, Standard is the best fit."

## 12. Call Flow

### Step 1: Frame the conversation

"I want to understand whether this is actually a fit for you, not force you into a plan."

### Step 2: Diagnose the bottleneck

Find out whether the problem is:

- not enough interviews
- weak interview performance
- poor positioning
- lack of consistency in applications

### Step 3: Re-state the gap

"What I am hearing is that you have the experience, but you are not converting that into enough real interview volume."

### Step 4: Match plan to need

Use urgency, seniority, and support needs to recommend the plan.

### Step 5: Close clearly

"Based on what you shared, I would recommend Standard because it gives you the 5-interview guarantee, 50 applications submitted, 1-on-1 support, and negotiation coaching without jumping straight to the Premium cost."

## 16. Rep Guardrails

- Do not invent case studies.
- Do not promise visa sponsorship.
- Do not promise offer count, offer amount, or hiring timeline beyond the published guarantee.
- Do not describe recruiter calls or casual chats as qualifying interviews.
- Do not change refund terms verbally.
- If a prospect asks about an edge case, say: "I want to keep that exact, so I would rather confirm against the written policy than guess."
