EMAIL DELIVERABILITY SETUP
Prevent emails from going to spam

STEP 1: ADD DNS RECORDS
Go to your domain registrar (GoDaddy) and add these DNS records for 100k-pathway.com:

SPF RECORD (Sender Policy Framework):
Type: TXT
Name: @
Value: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
(Replace with your SMTP provider's SPF if not using Gmail)

DKIM RECORD (DomainKeys Identified Mail):
Gmail: Enable in Gmail Admin Console, then add provided TXT record
Custom domain: Generate DKIM keys and add to DNS

DMARC RECORD (Domain-based Message Authentication):
Type: TXT
Name: _dmarc
Value: v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@100k-pathway.com

STEP 2: VERIFY DOMAIN OWNERSHIP
Add this TXT record to prove you own the domain:
Type: TXT
Name: @
Value: (Provided by your email service provider)

STEP 3: CONFIGURE REVERSE DNS (PTR)
Contact your hosting provider to set PTR record pointing to your domain

STEP 4: EMAIL BEST PRACTICES
1. Use authenticated domain (noreply@100k-pathway.com not gmail.com)
2. Warm up: Start with 20 emails/day, increase gradually
3. Monitor bounce rate (keep under 5%)
4. Include unsubscribe link (already added)
5. Avoid spam trigger words: free, urgent, click here, etc.
6. Send from consistent IP/domain

STEP 5: TEST DELIVERABILITY
Use these tools to check your setup:
- mail-tester.com (score should be 10/10)
- mxtoolbox.com/SuperTool.aspx
- Google Postmaster Tools

GMAIL SETUP (Easiest):
1. Go to myaccount.google.com/apppasswords
2. Generate app password
3. Add to .env:
   SMTP_USER=your-email@gmail.com
   SMTP_PASS=generated-app-password
   FROM_EMAIL=your-email@gmail.com

Gmail automatically handles SPF/DKIM, scores 10/10 on deliverability tests.
